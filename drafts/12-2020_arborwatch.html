CASEY TREES TESTIFIES!

On December 8, Casey Trees testified at the Committee on Transportation and the Environment regarding bill B23-0922 - The Public Tree Protection and Canopy Expansion Amendment Act. The goal of this bill is to expand tree protection to include all District trees. Unfortunately some of the provisions, like allowing an agency to only plant two new trees when they removed a Special tree, could have weakened the existing tree law.

Now that the first hearing is complete, the Committee aims to adapt and revise the bill based on our feedback and that of our partners to create a bill that protects District trees without harming the existing Urban Forestry Preservation Act.

DID YOU KNOW...

You may recognize maple trees from your local tree box or the Canadian flag, but there is more to this tree than it may seem. The maple tree family, Aceraceae, is one of the oldest, with fossils of maple trees dating back at least 100 million years. And, while they are known for making some of the best syrup, the wood of a maple tree is also one of the best for musical instruments because of its ability to carry sound waves. This fast growing species is loved by both pollinators and people. In fact, the Iroquois tribe has a festival dedicated to maple and in the Osaka area of Japan, there is a snack made from cured and fried maple leaves.
