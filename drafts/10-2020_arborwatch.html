CASEY TREES / ARBORWATCH UPDATE

10/2020

COMPREHENSIVE PLAN COMMENTS TO THE COUNCIL

The Comprehensive Plan is in its final stages! You may remember back in October 2019, the Office of Planning submitted their drafts of the different Comprehensive Plan elements. After a 4 month comment period where residents, organizations and ANCs were able to provide feedback on the draft, the Mayor submitted her amendments to the draft Comprehensive Plan. Now, it is the D.C. Council’s turn!

How do we feel about this new version?

We feel good! The Administration’s amendments kept the strong focus on building resiliency throughout the District using green techniques, such as adding trees and maintaining existing open spaces. It promotes expanding our urban forest, preserving our existing green spaces and using environmental and green designs to increase health and wellness of communities. The new version also has updated language to fit with existing policies (such as the new measurements for Special trees) and includes extra language about adding trees as part of uniform streetscape design and to help create welcoming and active public spaces.

You can read more about the newest draft of the Comprehensive plan or our full review of it from the May ArborWatch.

So what's next?

On November 12, the D.C. Council will begin their first of two hearings to review and approve the final version of the Comprehensive Plan. SUBMIT COMMENTS TODAY to ask the Council to support the continued growth and preservation of our tree canopy. Comments are due December 4, 2020.

READY 2 PLAY D.C.

The Department of Parks and Recreation (DPR) is starting the process of creating a new parks master plan called Ready 2 Play D.C. Right now, DPR staff are gathering information about what different parks need, and we want to help them!

We know you know about your local parks and what makes them great, but we challenge you to go and visit a new park this month and tell us what you think. How is it different from your usual park? What do you like or not like about it? Is there something this park has that you think would be good in your park? Is there something your park has that you think could benefit the park you visited?

The only rule is that the park must be D.C. owned (because it is a D.C. plan). Explore this map of all District Parks!
