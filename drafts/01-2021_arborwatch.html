PERFORMANCE OVERSIGHT HEARINGS START NEXT WEEK

The 2021 Performance Oversight hearings start on January 25th, and we want YOU to submit comments! These hearings are the only time that the Council reviews an entire year’s worth of agency activities. Submitting comments or testifying lets our Councilmembers know where these agencies have done well, and where they need to improve. Equally important, testifying and/or submitting comments helps keep relationships with the agencies and the Council strong and productive for current and future initiatives.

Share your thoughts, experiences and advice and support for increasing protection, adding more trees and greenspace, and supporting green infrastructure across the entire District! These comments will help the agencies improve their services in the new year and guide the Council on future funding decisions.

This year, all verbal testimonies will be done virtually. SIGN UP to present your testimony. The hearings will be held:

  • DOEE – February, 22; 9:00 AM
  • DPR – February 23, 3:00 PM
  • DDOT – February 26, 12:00 PM
  • DGS – March 8, 12:00 PM
  • OZ – March 18, 12:00 PM
  • OP – March 18, 12:00 PM

Head to Casey Trees' Advocacy Portal for example comments, the Advocacy Handbook and Casey Trees’ 2021 Performance Oversight hearing recommendations. Comments are due 2 days before the agency hearing.

We look forward to reading them!

FUN TREE FACTS: TREES BY THE NUMBERS

We all know that there are 2.4 million trees in the District, but did you know that there are 12 times more trees on Earth than stars in the milky way? There are about 3 trillion trees on our planet and more than 60,000 species, with new ones still being found. However, trees are still relatively new by archeological standards – it was only in the last 385 million years that trees began to grow (the first ever tree-like plant was actually found in New York!). While many of these original tree species have died out, the ginkgo tree is considered one of the most ancient living trees, dating back about 200 million years.
